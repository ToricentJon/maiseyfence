Wasilla Fencing

Fencing for Wasilla's Booming Growth

Wasilla is the fastest-growing city in Alaska's Mat-Su Borough, and we've been here for every wave of that growth. From the original Parks Highway corridor to the newest subdivisions off Knik-Goose Bay Road, Maisey Fence has installed more fences in Wasilla than any other contractor.

What makes Wasilla fencing unique is the sheer volume of new construction. Unlike Anchorage where most fencing goes on established properties, a huge percentage of our Wasilla work is for brand-new homes. That means coordinating with builders, reading survey plats, and working around fresh landscaping and drainage plans — skills that take years to develop.

We also understand Wasilla's HOA landscape better than anyone. As subdivisions like Settlers Bay, Tanaina, Cottonwood Creek, and Knik Estates have grown, each has adopted its own set of CC&Rs governing fence height, material, color, and setback. We've worked with every major Wasilla HOA and know their requirements before we ever pull up to your lot.

New Construction

Wasilla New Build Fencing

More new homes are built in Wasilla each year than in any other Mat-Su Borough city. We've developed a streamlined process specifically for new construction fencing that saves builders and homeowners time and money:

  • Lot line verification — we locate survey pins and confirm property boundaries before any posts go in, avoiding costly neighbor disputes
  • Builder coordination — we schedule around concrete, landscaping, and final grading so our work doesn't interfere with other trades
  • HOA pre-approval — we submit fence plans to your HOA architectural review committee and handle all required approvals
  • Utility locate — we call 811 and verify underground utility locations on every new construction lot (critical with Wasilla's expanding infrastructure)
  • Volume pricing — builders developing multiple lots get competitive project pricing

If you're building a new home in Wasilla, bring us in early. We can price your fence during the design phase so it's included in your construction budget rather than being an expensive afterthought.

HOA Compliance

Wasilla HOA Fencing Experts

Wasilla's rapid growth has brought dozens of subdivisions with homeowner associations, each with its own fencing rules. Getting it wrong means tearing out your fence and starting over — an expensive mistake we've seen other contractors make.

Common Wasilla HOA requirements we navigate daily:

  • Material restrictions — some HOAs require specific materials (cedar only, no chain link, or ornamental iron in front yards)
  • Height limits — many HOAs limit fences to 4 or 5 feet even when the borough allows 6 feet
  • Color standards — stain colors, paint colors, or "natural only" requirements that vary by subdivision
  • Setback rules — fences set back 1-3 feet from property lines, not on them — unique to HOA communities
  • Architectural review — many Wasilla HOAs require pre-approval with drawings and material samples before any fence construction begins

We maintain files on every major Wasilla HOA's fence requirements. When you call us, we likely already know your subdivision's rules.

Local Knowledge

Wasilla Soil & Climate

Wasilla's lake-country geography creates fencing challenges that don't exist in Anchorage or even nearby Palmer:

  • High water tables — properties near Wasilla Lake, Lucille Lake, Finger Lake, and the Meadow Lakes area often have water just 2-3 feet below the surface in spring, which floods post holes and weakens concrete footings
  • Variable drainage — sandy glacial deposits drain instantly in some lots while clay pockets 100 feet away hold water all summer
  • Lake-effect microclimate — properties near lakes experience more fog, ice, and freeze-thaw cycles than inland lots just miles away
  • Frost depth — open Wasilla lots without tree cover can see frost penetration beyond 6 feet; posts must reach a minimum of 48 inches below grade
  • Moose traffic — Wasilla's low-density development means moose move freely through neighborhoods year-round; fence visibility and strength must account for moose impact

We've installed fences in every soil condition Wasilla has to offer. We test before we dig, and we adjust our approach lot by lot — not a one-size-fits-all operation.
