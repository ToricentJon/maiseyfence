Fence Types

Commercial Fence Options

High-Security Chain Link

The workhorse of commercial fencing in Alaska. Our commercial chain link installations use industrial-grade materials designed for decades of maintenance-free performance in extreme conditions:

  • 9-gauge wire fabric — the heaviest standard gauge, resists cutting and climbing
  • 2-7/8" diameter posts — SS40 schedule pipe, set 48+ inches deep
  • 8-foot post spacing — tighter than residential for maximum rigidity
  • Barbed wire arms — 3-strand angled outward for security deterrence
  • Razor ribbon option — BTO-22 concertina wire for high-security applications
  • Privacy slats or windscreen — blocks visibility into sensitive areas

Anti-Climb Panels

For facilities requiring the highest level of physical security, anti-climb mesh panels provide near-impenetrable barriers:

  • 358 mesh pattern — 3" x 0.5" openings too small for finger or toe holds
  • Welded construction — cannot be pried apart like woven chain link
  • Powder-coated finish — corrosion protection for coastal Anchorage environments
  • 8-10 foot heights — standard for correctional and high-security facilities

Additional commercial options include ornamental iron fencing for retail storefronts, bollard systems for vehicle barriers, and temporary panel fencing for construction sites and events.

Regulations

Compliance & Permitting

Commercial fencing in Anchorage involves regulations that residential projects don't. We handle the entire permitting process so you can focus on your business:

  • Municipality of Anchorage codes — Title 21 height limits, setback requirements, and material restrictions by zoning district
  • Barbed wire regulations — permitted in commercial/industrial zones but restricted near residential boundaries
  • ADA gate requirements — minimum 36-inch clear width, accessible hardware, level landing areas at gates
  • OSHA site security — construction site fencing requirements for fall hazards, excavations, and material storage
  • Fire department access — Knox box and emergency access gate requirements for AFD response
  • Utility easements — fencing around transformers, meters, and utility access points must maintain clearance requirements
